Summary

My Approach:
- The LLM can fail to kill trivial mutants, because it assumes that the experiment failed when the mutant raises an error.
- The LLM sometimes has an entirely wrong approach for detecting the mutant. This might be due to inherent randomness. E.g.
    - testing the wrong method
    - not supplying an optional parameter that's needed to detect the mutant
    - supplying a parameter that has to be omitted to detect the mutant

Baseline:
- The baseline approach almost always gets the imports wrong on the first try.
